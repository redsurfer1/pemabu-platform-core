# Incentive remediation — platform access allocations (v1)

**Status:** Active policy text (2026-04-02 — Sprint 4 clean model)  
**Related:** `Model-Card-V1.md`, `regulatory-scrub-report.md`

## Purpose

Document how **incentive** and **remediation** programs are framed so they are **not** read as:

- Stored-value balances the platform owes in cash, or  
- Discretionary **consumer credit** decisions.

## Definitions

| Term | Meaning after scrub |
|------|---------------------|
| **Platform access allocation** | **Non-monetary** recognition: badges, access tier bumps, temporary feature flags, or **reporting** scores. Any cash compensation must use a **separate**, counsel-approved program. |
| **Remediation** | Corrective **workflow** actions (e.g. re-run verification, human review, throttle automation) — **not** automatic cash payouts. |
| **Access allocation (legacy code)** | **Platform access capacity** only — concurrent jobs, API rate, automation depth — **not** lending. |

### Slash rate

The slash rate is a platform quality metric. It has no monetary value. A high slash rate reduces an operator's platform access allocation. No monetary penalty is assessed by the platform.

### 72-hour review period

During the 72-hour review period, the operator's platform access allocation is in review status. No monetary amount is at stake. The operator's access to platform features may be temporarily restricted pending metric improvement.

## LEGAL_REVIEW

- If marketing or contracts describe incentives as **cash entitlements** drawable on demand, treat as **LEGAL_REVIEW** until reworded.  
- Confirm incentives do not constitute **wages** or **securities** under applicable law without separate analysis.
